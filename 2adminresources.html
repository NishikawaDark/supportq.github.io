Resources

Counselor reference materials, crisis protocols, and student support guides

These resources are for counselor and admin reference. Student-facing resources are available on the student portal.

Crisis Protocols

Emergency Crisis Line

988

24/7 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. Advise students to call or text anytime for immediate, confidential support.

Call 988

Immediate Risk Protocol

If a student indicates they are in immediate danger, follow your school's escalation procedure.

1
Stay calm and do not leave the student alone.
2
Contact a school administrator or designated counselor immediately.
3
Call 911 if there is immediate physical danger.

Counselor Guidance

Trauma-Informed Communication

Use open-ended questions, validate feelings, and avoid minimizing language when speaking with a distressed student.

🗣️ Active listening ❓ Open questions 🤝 Validation 🚫 No judgment

Reading Check-in Data

When reviewing a student's check-in, look at mood trends over time — single low scores matter less than consistent patterns.

Tip: Use the Assessments tab to view a student's full history and identify patterns across multiple days.

Self-Care for Counselors

Supporting others is demanding. Ensure you're also accessing supervision, peer support, and regular breaks to maintain your own wellbeing.

🧘 Mindfulness 👥 Peer supervision 📅 Regular breaks

Admin Quick Reference

Responding to Flagged Students

When the dashboard flags a student as "Urgent" or "Needs Attention", here's the recommended workflow.

1
Open the student's latest check-in in the Assessments tab to review their scores and notes.
2
Send a message via the Contact Students tab to check in directly.
3
If the student indicates crisis, escalate using the protocols above.
4
Document all actions taken per your school's safeguarding policy.

Understanding the Risk Levels

The dashboard automatically categorizes students based on their latest check-in data.

Urgent — Mood ≤ 4, overwhelmed, or felt unsafe
Attention — Mood 5–6 or stress ≥ 7
Stable — Mood ≥ 7, low stress

Data Privacy & Confidentiality

All student check-in data and messages are strictly confidential. Access is limited to authorized counselors and administrators. Never share student information without explicit consent and in accordance with your institution's privacy policy.
